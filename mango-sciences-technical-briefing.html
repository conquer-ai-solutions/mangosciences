Organisation Context

A cancer data platform
with massive ambitions.

Mango Sciences sits at the intersection of oncology data, value-based drug financing, and clinical trials. They're not a typical health-tech startup — they have real data, real revenue mechanisms, and a clear need for agentic AI.

What Mango Sciences actually does

Cancer data platform with ~2.7 million lines of phenotypic clinical data, primarily acquired from India. They operate across two core use cases: (1) a value-based financing mechanism for cancer drugs where patients get a money-back guarantee based on health outcomes, and (2) clinical trials via an invested CRO partner, with their first two trials coming up.

The data challenge — this is their obsession

Data harmonisation is the central technical problem. Almost 3 million patients worth of extremely messy EHR records — even the structured data is messy. They're currently using ontology-based NLP approaches (inspired by Palantir's ontology-first philosophy) and are "wading into GenAI" cautiously.

They're about to go multimodal: genomics and radiology/pathology data are coming. The harmonisation challenge is only going to get harder.

Agentic readiness — their own framing

Hanif's team is focused on getting their data platform into "agentic readiness" — their term, not ours. They understand that letting loose agents on messy data doesn't work (they've tried). This is a sophisticated buyer who knows what they need but hasn't cracked the execution.

The flywheel ambition

Mohit's strategic goal is not building any single application — it's building the capability to rapidly create applications. He wants a platform approach to agentic AI. In his words: "The ability to build applications is more the goal than building an application in particular." This is exactly how we think about it.

Watch out — Distributed team: Mango's team is spread across Dubai, India, US West Coast, and US East Coast. Decision-making and technical collaboration will need to account for timezone complexity and distributed ownership.

Opportunity Areas

Four surfaces
for agentic AI.

Based on the March 16th discovery call, these are the areas where agentic AI could deliver the most value. Today's roadmap presentation should validate, refine, or reorder these.

Opportunity 01 — Likely Entry Point
Data Harmonisation & Agentic Readiness
The central problem: ~3M messy EHR records that need to be harmonised, with multimodal data (genomics, radiology/pathology) incoming. They're currently using ontology-based NLP and have tried (and failed with) agentic pipelines on raw data. The opportunity is helping them bridge the gap between their current ontology approach and agentic readiness — building the data foundation that makes everything else possible. This is their obsession and our most credible entry point.
Opportunity 02
Annotation Tooling & Non-Technical Interfaces
Mango has a non-technical annotation team that needs purpose-built interfaces to work with the data. They need annotation tools and a unified data control plane. This is a classic agentic AI application: build intelligent tooling that amplifies non-technical users, with agents handling the complex data operations underneath. Contained, measurable, high internal value.
Opportunity 03
Patient Similarity & Outcome Modelling
Gavin has done significant work on patient similarity analysis using traditional models. This connects directly to their value-based financing mechanism (money-back guarantee on cancer drugs based on outcomes). The agentic opportunity: augment traditional modelling with AI-driven cohort building and outcome prediction. Higher stakes (consequence of non-determinism matters here), but potentially the highest-value use case.
Opportunity 04
Clinical Trial Acceleration
First two clinical trials coming up via their invested CRO partner. We have direct experience in clinical trials (Tom referenced an existing client under NDA). The agentic opportunity: patient matching, site selection, protocol optimisation, regulatory document processing. This is a longer-term play but differentiates us — mention our clinical trial experience (within NDA constraints) to build credibility.
Sequencing logic: Opp 01 (data harmonisation) is the foundation everything else depends on. It's also where they're spending the most effort and have the most pain. Lead here, use it to prove our approach, and let it naturally open Opps 02–04. Each subsequent engagement builds on the data platform we help them establish.
The flywheel play: Mohit doesn't want us to build one application — he wants the capability to rapidly create applications. If we position our work as building the agentic platform (Opp 01 + 02), not just solving one problem, we align with his strategic vision and become a long-term partner, not a one-off vendor.
Our Approach

Listen first.
Then whiteboard.

Today is about understanding, not proposing. Let Hanif and Gavin present. Probe where it matters. Leave with enough context to produce an alignment doc or whiteboard as the next step.

How this meeting should flow

Phase 1 — Mango presents (30 min): Hanif and Gavin walk through the product roadmap, agentic AI use cases, and lessons from past attempts. Our job: listen, take notes, ask clarifying questions.

Phase 2 — We probe (15 min): For each use case, apply the non-determinism framework. Understand the full process around each opportunity. Push for measurable success criteria.

Phase 3 — Next steps (15 min): Propose the whiteboard as the next step. Offer to produce an alignment doc that reflects our understanding back to them. This is the Conquer way — earn the right to propose by demonstrating understanding first.

For each use case they present:

— What happens when the AI gets it wrong? What's the consequence of non-determinism here?

— What does the end-to-end process look like today? Where does this use case sit within it?

— What would success look like in measurable terms? What KPI moves?

— Have you tried anything here before? What worked, what didn't, and why?

For Hanif (data platform):

— Walk us through the current data architecture — what sits where, what's harmonised, what's still raw?

— When you say "agentic readiness" — what does that state look like to you? What needs to be true about the data before you'd trust an agent with it?

— How does the ontology-based NLP pipeline work today? Where does it break down?

For Gavin (data science):

— The patient similarity work — what models are you using, and where do you see the ceiling with the traditional approach?

— As multimodal data comes in (genomics, radiology), how does that change your modelling approach?

— What does the annotation team's workflow look like? Where do they spend the most time?

Tom's headline framework from the first call, and the single most important lens for evaluating Mango's use cases:

"The absolute first question I always ask myself is: what is the consequence of non-determinism?"

For a given input, a language model can't guarantee a specific output. So for each use case:

1. What happens if the AI is wrong? Is it a minor inconvenience or a patient safety issue?

2. If we need human-in-the-loop, does that delete the value? If HITL reduces the ROI to near-zero, it might not be the right starting point.

3. Can we scope the use case so the consequence of error is contained? Data harmonisation errors can be caught and corrected. Clinical outcome predictions that are wrong can have severe consequences.

This framework will help us rank their use cases by viability, not just desirability.

Mohit's exact words on the goal: "The ability to build applications is more the goal than building an application in particular — how do we get the flywheel started rather than just build the wheels."

Hanif on data: "It's a hell of a job, this data thing. Almost 3 million patients, billions of very messy EHR records. We're going to be multimodal pretty soon — genomics and radiology/pathology."

Muna's strategic point: "You are relatively early in your journey, and this might be exactly the right time to build from scratch rather than layering onto legacy processes." — This reinforces our platform approach.

What they've tried: "We already tried some and it doesn't work great — let loose a bunch of somewhat well-defined agentic pipelines on top of very messy data." — Probe what specifically went wrong.

Risks & Watch Points

Things to keep
front of mind.

Watch out — Data sensitivity in oncology: Cancer patient data is among the most sensitive data categories globally. Any solution we propose must account for regulatory compliance (HIPAA, Indian data protection laws, and potentially GDPR for ex-US markets). Acknowledge this upfront — it builds trust and shows we understand the domain.
Watch out — New CTO, new vision: Hanif is 6 months in. He's building his technical vision and team. Be careful not to position Conquer in a way that feels like we're stepping on his toes or implying his current approach is wrong. Frame our contribution as accelerating his roadmap, not replacing it.
Watch out — NDA still pending: The mutual NDA from the first call hasn't been formally completed (at least not in our system). This may limit what they share today. Don't push for proprietary details they're not comfortable sharing yet — and if the NDA does come up, offer to move quickly on our side.
Don't solutionise too early: Tom was explicit about this in the first call. We don't know enough about their business yet. Today is about understanding. The temptation will be to hear a problem and immediately propose a solution — resist it. The right next step is a whiteboard or alignment doc, not a proposal.
Why this is a strategic account: Mango sits at the intersection of healthcare, AI, and clinical trials — three of the highest-value verticals for agentic AI. They have real data (2.7M patients), real revenue mechanisms (value-based financing), and a CEO who thinks in platform terms. If we can help them build the agentic flywheel, this becomes a flagship case study for healthcare AI and opens doors across pharma and biotech. Combined with Muna's network of biotech advisory clients, Mango could be the reference account that unlocks an entire sector.
